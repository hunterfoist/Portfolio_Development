Get to know me.

They call me Firefox. No, it’s not because I have an unrelenting passion for Mozilla (I use Chrome). Rather, it’s a name that was given to me six years ago when I joined Camp Kesem, a camp for children affected by a parent’s cancer. We all have camp names. Mine happened to be modeled on my reddish hair, my fox-like facial features, and my love of all things technology.

That love of technology has driven me to pursue a career where digital media and creativity would take the forefront. Having dual degrees in English education and advertising has allowed me to use my creativity to strategically plan and create advertising campaigns for national brands while also lesson planning for a group of rambunctious 9th graders. Naturally, I began experimenting with Photoshop and InDesign and found that I had a knack for digital creation and simple, sleek designs. My experience in the classroom is one of my biggest strengths. My speech has to be clear and my writing concise.

Constantly abiding to strict time constraints, I understand how to use my time wisely and operate with deadlines. I know my dual degree might seem like an odd combination, but creatively engaging the next major group of consumers in the classroom allows me to see behaviors of the next generation that will be vital for media, content, planning, and strategy.

In my experiences with Matchbook Creative and The Sandbox Agency, I’ve run the gambit from large multinational clients trying to increase market share to small start-up nonprofits just trying to get their name out there. I know that I can take that valuable experience and put it to use. So yeah, I may just be a red-headed, fox-like geek, but I’m a Firefox with a passion for learning and creativity. I want to thank you for taking the time to read this. I hope to hear from you soon.
